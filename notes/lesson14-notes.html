Summary for lesson 14

Having a single page web allows for all the information on the site to download quickly, the whole site can be printed on a single print page, visitors can save the site on their hard drives for future reference, and links between different parts on the same page may respond more quickly.

The goal of a website is to make other webpages visitable and act as a portal to access information with sites. A common mistake beginner web-designers make is that different pages look fundamentally different from each other. A good web design should strive for unifomity within the site itself. It should also adhear to a unique and unmistakable visual theme to make it stand out from all the other web sites on the internet.

In order to optimize websites for the search engine, you should note the following:

  • Interesting content—This is the most important feature of a search engine–friendly site. You need to have text, multimedia, and images that people want to read and view.
  • Easy-to-use navigation—You want visitors to your site to stay as long as they want, no matter what page they end up on.
  • Mobile friendliness—Mobile-friendly sites are critical for search engine placement. More and more people use mobile devices, and Google and other search engines are switching to an index that prioritizes mobile-friendly designs.

Reflection

This lesson highlights the importance of organizing websites with consistent design and clear navigation to ensure a cohesive user experience. Version control and frameworks like Bootstrap are introduced as tools to streamline development and improve efficiency, especially for larger projects.
